Group Contract - HIT-237

Project Theme: 
    - Remote Housing Crisis
        There is an extremely severe and ongoing housing crisis in many of Australia's remote and regional communities with regard to safe, sustainable, and affordable housing (disproportionately impacting vulnerable populations, including Indigenous people). There are numerous reasons contributing to this crisis, including the geographic isolation of these communities, the high costs of constructing and maintaining housing, insufficient or inadequate infrastructure in these communities, and the level of population growth that has occurred in remote areas in recent years. As a result of the above factors, individuals living in overcrowded conditions and without access to necessary services face additional social, economic, and other issues.

Section-1
    Terms and Condition of Group Work

        Group Allocation
            Group members will be allocated grades on the basis of how they contribute to any group assignments (each member will contribute an equal amount of effort). The final grade will be subject to adjustment based upon the degree of contribution that has been verified (as measured by GitHub commits, active participation in Microsoft Teams discussions and/or attendance at meetings, as well as the number of CLF Member Points earned). If you (as a member) do not provide effort consistently over the course of the assignment or fail to meet deadlines or turn in work that is below quality after being warned will be subject to have their grade fall below what was deserved.

        Group Communication
            Group members will communicate through Microsoft Teams and GitHub Issues for official purposes. All members must respond to messages from each other within 24 hours of being sent. There will be weekly meetings scheduled based on when all members are able to attend; those weekly meetings will be discussed and booked using Microsoft Teams. All meeting minutes will be kept and stored using Microsoft Teams. Important decisions made at meetings should also be documented.

        Task Ownership / Marks Allocation
            Tasks will be assigned using Team meetings, which involves us discussing in a group and effectively allocating proper tasks to every group members. Each group member will be responsible for completing assigned tasks by the due date that has been agreed upon. But if a group member:
            - has not completed (missed) 1 due date - they will receive a reminder

            - has not completed (missed) 2 due dates - that task will be reassigned to someone else

            - has not contributed for more than 48 hours - then, their work will be redistributed; documented and will be reported to the CLF.

        Diverse Work Styles
            The group recognizes that members have different schedules/appointments. A member’s availability and strengths (skills) will determine the assignment of tasks. Members must communicate to the group in advance of any anticipated delays. Flexibility will only be considered if communicated in advance.We have made the times for meeting and actually doing the assigned task quite flexible, so which will lead to a proper and timely completion of all task with efficiency.
        
        Resolution of Conflict
            While we do not think the team composition will be a problem or we are going to have any major conflicts, but if there are any arising we will be following four steps to resolving those arising conflicts using the following measures;
            - Group conversation about conflict
            - If unresolved, majority rule will apply
            then if it gets worse
            - Escalate to CLF (Shantanu Barua)
            - Only escalate to unit manager if needed
            All conflicts and their resolutions must be documented.
        
        Academic Integrity
            All work completed by group members must be in accordance with university policy. The use of AI technology requires:
            - All members to be given access
            - All members to have read it thoroughly
            - All members to have reviewed/edited it together and included
            - All members to have properly credited the creator of the work used. 
            If a member is found guilty of breaking university policy by plagiarizing other people’s work, he/she will be referred for discipline.

        Professionalism and Participation by Group Members
            All group members are required to act professionally in meetings and electronically. Member points for CLF (Participation, Timeliness, Quality) will impact each person’s individual grade and member points will be documented via meeting recordings, minutes of meetings.
